Insider knowledge

Five tips that will save you time, money and a few raised eyebrows

Tap a tip to expand it.

1. Get an Oyster Card or Use Contactless

Cash payments for transit are costly and inconvenient. For the cheapest tickets on the Tube, buses, and most rail services, use a contactless card or device, or pick up a Visitor Oyster card.

2. Stand on the Right

Always stand on the right side of escalators on the London Underground so passengers in a hurry can ascend on the left. Londoners take this unspoken rule very seriously.

3. Explore Beyond Zone 1

Central London (Zone 1) holds the famous landmarks, but the outer zones reward exploration. Try Richmond, Hampstead Heath, or Greenwich for a different view of the city.

4. Check for Free Museums

The Tate Modern, the National Gallery and the British Museum are among the world-class institutions that offer free admission to permanent collections. Special exhibitions are usually ticketed.

5. Tipping is Not Always Necessary

Restaurants frequently add a 12.5% service charge automatically. Tipping beyond that isn't standard practice. For cabs, it's polite to round up the fare.
